Some people assume that Information Security started with the Mainframe computers of the 1950s. However, we can go back over 2,000 years to the time when Julius Caesar ruled Rome in order to see early information security technology. Julius Caesar used something called the “Caesar Cipher” to send encoded secret messages to distant outposts of the Roman Empire.

During the 19th Century (1801 to 1900) when postal communication was starting to grow in volume and scope, governments started to develop procedures for guarding important communications. These were largely physical protections (safes and lock boxes, guards).

In the 20th Century (1901-2000) Information Security developed most rapidly in response to the two World Wars, where increasingly large and complex militaries needed to protect increasingly important and complex communication structures. One famous example of the use of Cryptography was the German Enigma machine used during World War II. This was famously cracked by British forces (led by the famous Computer Scientist, Alan Turing) leading to a considerable advantage for the Allied Forces side of the war.

Now in the 21st Century (2001 to the present) Information Security has grown massively in importance as the world more and more relies on electronic networks (mainly the Internet) for so many essential services.

Research Overview

Information security research studies the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information in order to protect it from misuse. Information must be confidential such that access is restricted to intended recipients and users. In addition, it must also maintain its integrity, so that it is not corrupted or misconstrued. Finally, information must have availability so that security practices do not infringe on its ability to be transferred and used13.

The importance of this research correlates with increasing amounts of accessed, exchanged, and stored information. In addition, there is a lack of education and awareness of information security. This research develops practices that protect billions of users and deters both cyberwarfare and cybercrime. Finally, it takes the role of establishing precedents and security policies for an increasingly digitized world.

The scope of information security research ranges from surgically specific (analyzing a specific security mechanism for cryptocurrency) to prolifically applicable, like analyzing how people are manipulated by social engineering. It can also go from abstract (mathematical algorithms for cryptography) to real world applications, like how investment banks use financial cryptography.
